People with motor disabilities are unable to communicate effectively with their care givers and family. Human Computer Interfaces (HCI) is being developed specifically for such people with disabilities such as injured-vertebra, apoplexy or poliomyelitis, Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis, and Motor Neuron Disease. HCI systems can rehabilitate such patients to communicate their needs to their care givers. An EOG based HCI is developed in this research.
